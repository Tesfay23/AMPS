A seven-item bedside score built for Ethiopian ICUs — validated pilot performance, now heading into a five-site external validation study to test whether it holds up beyond where it was born.

The gap

Built where the beds run out

Prognostic scores used worldwide were largely developed in high-income ICUs, where critical-care capacity looks nothing like Ethiopia's. AMPS was built to work without advanced labs, in settings like these.

The instrument

Seven signs, scored at the bedside

Each variable is binary — present (1) or absent (0) — assessed within 6 hours of ICU admission where possible. No labs beyond a basic panel are required.

What success looks like

Objectives & expected outcomes

Specific objectives
  • Evaluate AMPS's accuracy, discrimination (AUROC), and calibration (Hosmer–Lemeshow) at admission and 48 hours.
  • Assess feasibility, barriers, and facilitators of routine use from ICU physicians' and nurses' perspectives.
  • Compare performance across five institutionally diverse sites to test generalizability.
Expected outcomes
  • A validated, low-resource mortality prediction tool suited to Ethiopian ICUs.
  • Evidence-based insight into real-world adoption challenges and enablers.
  • A foundation for wider adoption and regional adaptation across similar LMIC critical-care settings.
